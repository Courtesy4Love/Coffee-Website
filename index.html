About iBasura

Many communities in the Philippines, particularly barangays in Lucban, Quezon, struggle with inappropriate garbage disposal. Common issues include illegal dumping, inadequate segregation methods, and ineffective collection schedules. These challenges persist despite efforts from local governing bodies, often due to reliance on manual processes and a lack of widespread awareness.

iBasura is a web-based waste management and reporting system specifically designed to address these challenges in the Lucban community. Our platform aims to bridge the communication gap between citizens and barangay authorities by leveraging digital technology. We focus on community involvement and education to foster sustainable waste management practices.

Our Goals:

  • Provide residents with an easy-to-use web platform for reporting and information.
  • Enable barangay officials to respond in real-time to waste-related concerns.
  • Educate users on proper waste segregation and recycling practices through accessible digital materials.
  • Implement a QR-code based logging system for waste pickup accountability and real-time data tracking.

Waste Education Hub

Proper Waste Segregation

Learn how to correctly segregate your waste into Biodegradable, Non-Biodegradable, and Recyclable categories. Proper segregation is the first step towards effective waste management.

  • Biodegradable (Nabubulok): Kitchen waste, food scraps, yard waste.
  • Non-Biodegradable (Di-Nabubulok): Plastics, styrofoam, residual waste.
  • Recyclable (Nareresiklo): Paper, cardboard, glass bottles, metal cans.

Composting & Recycling Tips

Discover simple ways to compost organic waste at home and best practices for recycling materials. Reduce landfill waste and contribute to a healthier environment.

  • Start a compost pit/bin for fruit/vegetable peelings.
  • Clean recyclables before disposal.
  • Know what your local MRF (Materials Recovery Facility) accepts.

QR Code Logging System

The iBasura system incorporates a QR-code based logging mechanism to enhance waste pickup accountability and provide real-time data. This innovative approach aims to streamline the collection process and ensure transparency.

How it Works:

  • For Residents: Households may be provided with unique QR codes. When waste is ready for collection, this QR code can be presented or scanned. (Specific implementation details will be provided by the Barangay).
  • For Collectors: Waste collection teams will be equipped with scanners. Scanning the QR code at the time of pickup logs the collection event, timestamp, and location.
  • Real-time Data: This data is transmitted in real-time (or near real-time) to the barangay's central system, allowing for efficient monitoring and management of waste collection routes and schedules.

Benefits:

  • Accountability: Ensures that collections are made as scheduled and recorded accurately.
  • Efficiency: Helps optimize collection routes and identify areas with missed pickups quickly.
  • Transparency: Provides both residents and barangay officials with clear data on waste collection activities.
  • Improved Service: Facilitates quicker response to missed collections and helps in planning better waste management strategies.

Instructions for residents on how to obtain and use their QR codes will be disseminated by the Barangay officials once the system is fully implemented.
